Game Overview

One Floor. Six kinds of trouble.

A 10×10 zigzag grid, the same wrap-around logic as a classic snakes-and-ladders board, but wired for an energy economy. Every cell type changes your position, your energy, or both.

Doors — 50 cells

Alternating Scarer and Laugher doors. Match your role and your whole team gains energy; mismatch and the whole team pays for it. One-time use, then exhausted forever.

Monster Cells — 6 cells

Home to the 6 monsters not in play. Land with a matching role and fire your powerup for free; land opposed and risk an energy swap.

Conveyor Belts — 5 cells

Automated canister-line transport that rockets you forward across the Floor, snakes-and-ladders style.

Contamination Socks — 5 cells

CDA emergency protocol. Sends you backward and drains 100 energy — unless a shield is holding.

Card Cells — 10 cells

Draw from a shuffled 25-card pile and resolve its effect on both monsters. Discarded after use, reshuffled when the pile runs dry.

Normal Cells — 24 cells

Quiet corridor space. Nothing happens here — which, on this Floor, is its own kind of relief.

How to Play

One turn, six moving parts.

Every turn on the Floor runs the same sequence, in this order, every time.

01

Powerup phase (optional)

Spend 500 energy to activate your monster's powerup before you move, if you can afford it.

02

Roll the dice

A standard six-sided roll sets how many cells you advance this turn.

03

Attempt the move

If your landing cell is occupied by the opponent, the move is invalid and you roll again.

04

Resolve the cell

Doors, cards, transports, and monster cells all fire their effect the moment you land.

05

Update the board

Positions and energy totals are finalized for both monsters.

06

Pass the turn

Unless the win condition is already met, the Floor hands control to the other monster.

Win Condition

You win the moment both are true at once — reaching short of either one just means the Floor keeps going.

Position = Cell 99 (Boo's Door)
Energy ≥ 1000
No shortcuts to Boo's Door

Arrive early without the energy and you're just another monster waiting for a better roll.

Meet the Monsters

Four philosophies. Eight canisters.

Every monster belongs to one of four types, each with its own passive trait and a powerup that costs 500 energy — or fires free when you land on a matching Monster Cell. Tap a card to see the mechanics.

Dasher

"Get to the door, get the laugh or scare, get out."

Tap for mechanics →
Passive — Lightning Movement

Base dice movement is doubled.

Powerup — Momentum Rush

3x movement speed for the next 3 turns, replacing the passive doubling while active.

Dynamo

"Maximum collection, maximum power."

Tap for mechanics →
Passive — Energy Amplification

All energy gains and losses are doubled. A double-edged sword by design.

Powerup — Energy Freeze

Freezes the opponent, forcing them to skip their entire next turn.

Multitasker

"Slow and steady. Handle everything perfectly."

Tap for mechanics →
Passive — Movement / Energy Trade-off

Dice movement is halved, but every energy gain or loss gets a flat +200 bonus.

Powerup — Focus Mode

Move at normal speed for 2 turns while keeping the +200 energy bonus.

Schemer

"Every setback is an opportunity."

Tap for mechanics →
Passive — Energy Manipulation

Every energy change gets a +10 bonus — gains grow, losses shrink.

Powerup — Chain Attack

Steals 10 energy from every other monster on the Floor at once, ignoring shields.
